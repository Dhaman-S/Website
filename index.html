Ferns

Ferns have been around for at least 360 million years, with fossil evidence indicating their presence during the time of dinosaurs. They are often used as biofertilizers, ornamental plants, and for remediating contaminated soil.

Mosses

They were the first plants to successfully colonize solid ground, dating back 450 million years. These useful plants have been used as bandages during World War I, and some species are even bioluminescent.

Conifers

Conifers are the oldest seed plants, evolving about 300 million years ago. Conifers are crucial for timber production and provide shelter for wildlife. Most conifers are both male and female, producing both male pollen and female cones.

Flowers

There are over 300,000 species of flowering plants on Earth. Flowering plants are the primary source of food for humans and animals, providing essential medicines and other resources.
